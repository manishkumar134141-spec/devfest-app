How It Works

ReviewShield uses a multi-layer AI pipeline to detect fake reviews with high accuracy. Here's how each step works.

1
🔗 URL Detection & Platform Identification
When you paste a product URL, our system instantly identifies which platform it belongs to — Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, Myntra, Snapdeal, etc. — and adapts the scraping strategy accordingly. The UI also dynamically changes its theme to match the platform.
2
📥 Review Extraction
We fetch all publicly available reviews for the product, including reviewer names, ratings, dates, verified purchase status, and the full review text. We collect up to 500 reviews per product for comprehensive analysis.
3
🤖 NLP & Language Analysis
A fine-tuned large language model analyzes each review for: generic or templated language, excessive superlatives, unnatural sentence structures, keyword stuffing (likely incentivized), copy-pasted content, and emotional authenticity.
4
👤 Reviewer Behavior Profiling
Each reviewer's profile is evaluated: account creation date, total review history, rating distribution (do they only give 5-stars?), whether they review many different product categories, and cross-platform identity matching.
5
📊 Verdict & Report
All signals are combined into a Fake Score (0-100). A score above 60 indicates heavily manipulated reviews. You get a breakdown of fake, suspicious, and genuine reviews, along with the specific reasons why each review was flagged.
Detection Signals We Analyze
💬
Generic Language
Overly positive, vague, or templated phrases used by fake reviewers
⏱️
Review Bursts
Many reviews appearing on the same day — classic review bombing
⭐
Rating Bias
Unnatural concentration of 5-star or 1-star ratings vs typical distributions
🔁
Duplicate Content
Same phrases or paragraphs appearing across multiple reviews
👻
New Accounts
Reviewers with accounts created days before their review
🛒
Unverified Purchase
High ratio of non-verified purchase reviews is a red flag
🧬
Behavioral Anomaly
Unusual reviewing patterns that don’t match normal user behavior
🕵️
Reviewer Network
Groups of accounts posting similar reviews — possible coordinated activity
